Title: Surya Namaskar (Sun Salutation)
Category: Asana Sequence
Level: Beginner to Intermediate

Overview:
Surya Namaskar is a sequence of yoga postures performed in a flowing manner.
It combines movement with breath and is often used as a warm-up or complete
practice on its own.

When This Practice Is Useful:
- As a full-body warm-up
- To improve overall flexibility and strength
- As a daily energizing routine

When To Avoid / Use Caution:
- Recent injury or surgery
- Severe joint pain
- Pregnancy (modify under guidance)

Key Benefits:
- Improves flexibility and circulation
- Strengthens major muscle groups
- Enhances coordination and stamina

Practice Notes:
Movements should be smooth and synchronized with breathing. Speed should be
adjusted based on comfort and experience.

Common Questions Answered:
- Can beginners practice Surya Namaskar? Yes, slowly.
- Is it enough as a full practice? Yes, for many people.

Related Practices:
- Tadasana
- Adho Mukha Svanasana
- Bhujangasana
